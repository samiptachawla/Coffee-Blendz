Cold Brew Recipe

Origin and Details

Cold Brew is a coffee brewing method that involves steeping coarse coffee grounds in cold water for an extended period, typically 12-24 hours. It produces a smooth, low-acid coffee concentrate that is served chilled or over ice.

Recipe

To make Cold Brew:

  1. Grind coffee beans to a coarse consistency.
  2. Combine the coffee grounds and cold water in a large jar or pitcher.
  3. Stir to ensure all the coffee grounds are fully saturated.
  4. Cover and refrigerate the mixture for 12-24 hours.
  5. Strain the cold brew concentrate through a fine-mesh sieve or coffee filter.
